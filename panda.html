PANDA

Pandas are large, bear-like mammals known for their distinctive black and white markings. They are native to China and are considered national treasures.

These adorable creatures primarily feed on bamboo, which makes up 99% of their diet. Pandas have a specialized thumb-like wrist bone, which helps them grasp bamboo stalks. Despite being classified as carnivores, pandas have a digestive system similar to that of herbivores.

Pandas have a peaceful and gentle demeanor. They spend most of their time eating and sleeping. Due to their low-calorie diet, they have a relatively low energy level and prefer to conserve their energy.

Unfortunately, pandas are an endangered species, with a small population remaining in the wild. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their natural habitat and ensure their survival.
